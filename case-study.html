Detailed Evaluation Report

Evaluating remedial education outcomes in Lebanon's refugee response programs

My Role

MEAL Coordinator

Focus Area

Education & Safeguarding

Location

Bekaa Valley, Lebanon

Key Tools

KoboToolbox • PowerBI • SPSS

1. Context & Challenge

The Bekaa Valley in Lebanon hosts a high concentration of Syrian refugee families. Local educational facilities operate in double-shift systems, leaving many children with gaps in basic literacy and math. The NGO launched a remedial education project targeting dropout reduction, but struggled with data fragmentation.

Our challenge was to:

  • Standardize reporting metrics across 8 separate local community centers.
  • Address community complaints securely without exposing vulnerable beneficiaries.
  • Deliver clean, real-time metrics to international donors (UNHCR/UNICEF).

2. MEAL Framework Design

We designed a structured Logical Framework (LogFrame) that mapped inputs directly to intermediate outcomes, establishing data collection responsibility at each program stage.

Project Output Indicator Description Baseline Target Verification Source
Remedial education support % of enrolled children attending regularly (>85% attendance) 58% 85% Monthly Attendance Database
Literacy & numeracy sessions % increase in baseline vs endline standardized literacy score N/A +30% score Pre/Post Evaluation Exams

3. Data Collection & Cleaning

We discarded paper sheets and deployed a centralized KoboToolbox mobile database setup.

Technology Spotlight: XLSForm Constraints

I scripted custom skip-logic structures and validation boundaries (e.g., ensuring a student's age matches the range of 6-14, preventing date registration errors). This reduced raw data input error rates from 18% to under 2%.

We coordinated training for 12 local enumerators in survey ethics, emphasizing consent procedures, gender-sensitive interview techniques, and secure data handling standards.

4. Analysis & Visualization

Using SPSS, I ran descriptive cross-tabulations on pre-test and post-test scores. This showed a statistically significant improvement in reading and arithmetic scores across the target cohorts.

I synced the Kobotoolbox database directly with PowerBI to compile automated dashboards showing:

  • Daily classroom attendance rates mapped against geographic center locations.
  • Progress curves of students toward the donor indicators.
  • Average resolution speed of community feedback cards.

5. Accountability & Adaptation

To ensure accountability to affected populations (AAP), we established confidential suggestion boxes in classrooms and a dedicated, toll-free MEAL helpline.

During month three, dashboard trends flagged an attendance drop at a specific center in Western Bekaa. Through review of qualitative feedback from suggestion cards, we discovered a lack of safe transportation during winter rainfall was causing the drop. In coordination with project management, we rerouted school buses, and attendance immediately recovered back to 87%.

Key Results

  • 2,500+ students monitored
  • Dropout rates down by 14%
  • 100% feedback cards tracked
  • Daily PowerBI syncing
  • 92% helpline tickets resolved

Methodologies

  • Quantitative pre/post testing
  • Focus Group Discussions (FGD)
  • Key Informant Interviews
  • Safeguarding Protocols (AAP)
  • LogFrame Matrix Design
